# Capstone Research Notes: Using AI

## Overview
Throughout my ASE capstone project, I have leveraged AI tools as a research partner, coding assistant, and brainstorming aid. This document summarizes key lessons, strategies, and reflections from integrating AI into my workflow.

## How I Used AI
- **Technical Research:** Used AI to quickly compare libraries, frameworks, and algorithms (e.g., threading models, Unreal subsystems, analysis tooling).
- **Debugging:** Asked AI to help diagnose errors, suggest fixes, and explain cryptic error messages.
- **Prototyping:** Generated code snippets for new features, then iteratively refined them based on project needs.
- **Learning New Concepts:** Used AI to explain advanced topics (e.g., GPU acceleration, multi-threading, real-time performance trade-offs) in accessible terms.
- **Best Practices:** Consulted AI for architectural advice, code organization, and performance optimization tips.

## What I Learned
- **AI as a Research Accelerator:** AI dramatically speeds up the process of surveying options, understanding trade-offs, and finding relevant documentation.
- **Critical Thinking is Essential:** AI suggestions are not always correct or optimal. I learned to treat AI as a starting point, always validating and adapting its output.
- **Iterative Development:** Combining AI-generated code with manual testing and debugging leads to faster, more robust solutions.
- **Documentation and Reflection:** Keeping notes on what worked (and what didn’t) helps build a personal knowledge base and avoid repeating mistakes.
- **Staying Current:** AI can surface recent research, libraries, and techniques that might not appear in traditional search results.

## Challenges
- **Ambiguity:** Sometimes AI responses are too generic or miss project-specific context, requiring careful prompting and follow-up questions.
- **Over-Reliance:** It’s tempting to lean too much on AI; I had to balance AI input with independent problem-solving and code review.
- **Debugging AI Output:** Not all generated code works out of the box—understanding why and how to fix it is a key skill.

## Reflections & Next Steps
- AI is a powerful tool for accelerating learning and development, but it works best when paired with strong fundamentals and critical thinking.
- I plan to continue using AI for research and prototyping, while focusing on deepening my understanding of core concepts and building more complex systems independently.
